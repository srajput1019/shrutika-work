Sign-off Surface · HITL redesign for Norm Ai

Where an attorney signs off on the agent

Norm's core product moment: Supervisory AI flags a compliance agent's work, and a human attorney must decide, fast, whether to approve. This is a working prototype of that surface — built around three questions: what do they need to see, how is confidence shown, and how does the decision become a signal.

01 · SEE
The rule that fired, in one glance
The flagged phrase is highlighted in place, tied to the exact rule and its citation — no hunting across tabs. The reviewer reads the risk before reading the reasoning.
Proof: RSMS exception queue — a single view to monitor, investigate & resolve, −25% manual errors.
02 · TRUST
Confidence shown as a calibrated band
Not a lone percentage. A calibrated band tells the attorney how much to trust the score itself — high-stakes, low-confidence items are surfaced as "read closely," not rubber-stamped.
Proof: Swaayata — every autonomous action visible, reversible, explainable; a reasoning graph, not a verdict.
03 · SIGNAL
The decision teaches the system
Approve, edit, or reject — each becomes a labeled training example. Human sign-off is the deliverable, so every sign-off is also the data that makes the next agent draft better.
Proof: Innovation Hacks HITL — human input / override on AI decisions, made visible and measurable.
