The Chief of Staff decides what reaches the CEO — and what doesn't

Nooks sells "human judgment with AI execution" — the parallel dialer and Agent Workspace that let reps at HubSpot, Deel, and Rippling spend judgment where it counts and hand the busywork to agents. At 400+ people and 1,200+ customers, the CEO needs that same principle applied to their own week: which numbers arrive untouched, which decisions are theirs alone, and which problems have no owner yet. This is a concept for the operating cockpit a Chief of Staff would run — the CEO's Monday, distilled.

For each metric: does it reach the CEO untouched, or does the CoS handle it? Gating well is the whole job — surface everything and you've added noise; surface nothing and you've hidden a fire.

Every decision is either the CEO's alone or something the CoS can delegate with a clear owner. Route each one.

The CoS catch-all: cross-functional problems that fall between teams and have no owner. Assign each before it becomes a fire.

The most credible board slide a CoS can hand this CEO: are our own reps using our own product? Nothing sells Nooks like Nooks' GTM team living in it.

Three design decisions in here

1. The gate is the product. The cockpit's value isn't the metrics — it's the judgment of which ones reach the CEO. That decision is made visible and adjustable, because a CoS earns trust by getting it right.

2. Every decision leaves with an owner. Routing isn't "handled" — it's delegated to a named function with a due date, so nothing quietly falls back on the CEO next week.

3. The digest writes itself. As you route, the "CEO's Monday, distilled" line rebuilds into the 30-second version — the artifact the CEO actually reads.

Why I built this

The Chief of Staff seat is operating judgment plus systems. On my Qlarc capstone I co-led strategy, built the architecture and the financial model myself, and led a 5-person cross-functional team. On a UnitedHealthcare project I owned budget and ROI modeling and ran a 6-phase rollout across compliance, IT, and Palantir — the cross-functional coordination this seat lives on. And Nooks' own "human judgment with AI execution" is the thesis behind everything I design.

I'd want the first conversation to be about your current operating rhythm — what reaches you untouched today, and what shouldn't.
